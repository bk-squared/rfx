A waveguide is a hollow metal pipe that carries microwave energy from one place
to another, the way a pipe carries water. A well-made empty guide should pass the
wave straight through with almost no reflection. This case checks exactly that.

## The structure

![The hollow rectangular waveguide](/rfx/gallery/assets/waveguide_wr90/geometry.png)

A standard rectangular waveguide (the WR-90 size used across the X-band, roughly
8 to 12 GHz). The walls are metal and the inside is empty air. A wave is launched
in at one end and collected at the other.

## The physics

<img src="/rfx/gallery/assets/waveguide_wr90/fields.gif" alt="Field animation — wave propagating down the waveguide" width="100%" />

The wave enters and ripples cleanly down the length of the guide. Because the
pipe is empty and well-matched at both ends, the energy keeps moving forward
instead of echoing back.

## The result

![How much reflects vs how much passes](/rfx/gallery/assets/waveguide_wr90/sparams.png)

Across the whole band, the reflection is essentially zero — it matches the exact
answer for an ideal empty guide, where everything that goes in comes out the far
end.
